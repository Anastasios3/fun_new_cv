Summary

I started my career earning a Finance degree while working in the hospitality industry. After graduation, I combined my finance background with the communication and organisational skills I gained behind the bar, moving into hospitality management. In Greece, I served as Operations Manager at a large venue, tackling daily challenges.

During my mandatory military service, I joined the networks and IT department, handling helpdesk tasks such as documentation, hardware maintenance, and support. This experience sparked my interest in technology, so I switched careers. Once my service ended, I enrolled in a two-year Software & Web Development programme at a local vocational institute, gaining broad tech exposure.

In my second year of study, I became an IT Support Specialist at a local dental clinic. With the CEO's support, I built a new website, developed an inventory management platform, and upgraded almost all hardware using government grants—new servers, dental 3D scanners, a 3D printing farm, improved wiring, and updated CRMs.

After earning my diploma, I pursued a larger market and further study by enrolling at the University of London. I then moved to Denmark for an internship at a healthtech start-up, where I helped develop an MRI/radiologist platform, redeveloped an admin page for implant approvals and reapprovals, and introduced real-time notifications.

I'm now focused on completing my second bachelor's, settling in the Nordics, and gaining cloud certifications. Drawing on my hospitality, finance, and tech backgrounds, I aim to bridge communication between technical and non-technical teams, delivering impactful IT solutions.
